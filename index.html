About

Rooted in Baringo County, driven by women, and powered by agritech tools.

Our Model

We align breeding to the rains, prioritize herd health, and keep meticulous records—from lambing to sales. Our goal is a replicable, resilient model for arid and semi-arid lands.

Our Values

  • Integrity and transparency
  • Women’s economic empowerment
  • Environmental stewardship

Impact

We measure success by livelihoods improved and sustainable production.

Jobs & Training

On-farm roles for women/youth; hands-on training in modern livestock management.

Food Security

Steady supply of safe, quality lamb/mutton to local markets.

Knowledge Sharing

Templates, dashboards, and farmer guides to help others adopt best practice.

Products & Services

Market Lambs

Well-finished lambs, typically 4–6 months, available to butchers and events.

Breeding Stock

Selected ewe lambs and rams from Dorper × Red Maasai crosses.

Training Days

Women & youth sessions on herd health, record-keeping, and grazing management.
